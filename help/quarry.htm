Detecting quarry explosions

 

By mapping the ratio of daytime to nighttime number of events, a first order estimate of explosion contamination can be achieved. Explosions such as quarry blast preferably take place during daytime hours. Frequently, a peak in the histogram, of the number of events per hour (shown below) can give a first order approximation of when explosions take place. By mapping out this ration spatially, once can also get an idea of the location of the quarries. For details please refer to ftp://seismo.ethz.ch/pub/stefan/preprints/quarries.

 

From the seismicity map, one can initiate a quarry detection mapping. First, the grid-size needs to be defined analogous to all other grids (dx, dy, Ni). Next, the daytime (or for that matter, any hour selection) must be defined. An histogram of the events distribution is plotted (Figure below). The program automatically marks the bins that contain the top 40% rates per hour. By clicking on the selection menus, any combination of hours can be compared. Select ‘Go’ to start the mapping, and interactively select the grid dimensions from the seismicity map window.

 

 

 

The resulting map displays the ratio of high day/time to nighttime. High ratios (~Rq > 1.5, purple) indicate possible quarry contamination. Interactively, the time series of events and the hourly distribution at a particular node can be viewed.
