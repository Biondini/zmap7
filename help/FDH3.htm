3.            The Spatial Variations of D

 

For the spatial variations of the correlation dimension, the distribution is divided spatially in subsets. This is achieved by defining a cross-section through the study region on which a grid is placed. At each node of the grid a subset is defined as the x closest events to the node. A D-value is computed for each subset and plotted accordingly. The result is a map (depth versus distance) of the cross-section with the corresponding D-values, figure 3.1. Low D-values are illustrated in blue and high D-values are in red. A graph with the correlation integral curves of all subsets is automatically computed. The colors of the lines correspond to the correlation dimension of the subset.

In the Select menu there are various options that allow measuring the correlation dimension for a subset, which is manually chosen within the cross-section. The overlay option enables to compare the correlation integral between two selected subsets.

In the Maps menu the b-value Map option will show the corresponding b-value Map. The D versus b option plots the D-values in function of the corresponding b-values. The slope of the red line estimates the correlation between the two values and the green lines represent the standard deviation. The blue line represents a slope of 2. The D versus resolution option plots the D-values in function of the radius of the sampling sphere.

In the Display menu the Fix color (z) scale enables to select a color scale.
