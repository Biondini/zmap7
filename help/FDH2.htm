2.            The Correlation Dimension

 

The correlation dimension of the selected distribution is calculated with the slope method. The result is a double-logarithmic plot of the correlation integral versus the distance (Figure 2.1). The red dots represent the points within the scaling range and with which the least squares fit is operated. The slope of the blue line corresponds to the D-value. The green lines illustrate the standard deviation.

 

 

 

Figure 2.1: Correlation integral curve for a distribution of 4000 points arranged in a Sierpinski Gasket geometry. The red dots illustrate the points in the scaling range, the slope of the blue line is the correlation dimension D and the green lines represent the standard deviation.

 

 

 

 

ZMAP

 

The computation is started from the Cumulative Number Window Figure(2.2):

 

 

 

Figure 2.2: Cumulative Number window. It is called from the ZTools menu of the Seismicity Map window.

 

 

 

a.             In the Tools menu, click on Fractal dimension estimation

b.             Select the option: Compute the fractal dimension D.

 

 

The resulting correlation integral curve is plotted in a window as shown in figure 2.1. With the Scaling range button, it is possible to change the scaling range by manually picking the extremes of the interval. By activating the Slope test button, the first derivative between each two points of the correlation integral is calculated and plotted versus the logarithmic distance. The red line represents the selected scaling range.
