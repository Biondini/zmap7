FIGURE 75. Schematic illustration of the clustering parameter of SEISMOLAP, S1. Zschau defines the overlap of the influence zone of earthquakes at positions Ei with the influence zone of a potential future earthquake located at P as the overlap of the four-dimensional cubes Ci(X, Y, Z, Tlap) formed around both Ei and P. The map view of the spatial cubes is shown in the map, and the overlap in time for an earthquake Ei with a particular window position of Tlap is shown at the bottom. The spatial cube around P, the location to be evaluated for earthquake potential, remains stationary throughout the experiment, while cubes in different positions Ei (the epicenters in the catalog) appear and disappear as a function of time. 91

FIGURE 76. The parameter S2 of SEISMOLAP, expressed as the probability (-1/the numbers on the ordinate) with which S2 is expected to occur, plotted as a function of time for three cases: (a) Landers (1992, M7.2), (b) Northridge (1994, M6.7), and (c) Chalfant Valley (1986, M6.2). A value of e.g. -500 indicates that the probability to observe this quiescence is -1/500 = 0.2%. In each case the SEISMOLAP parameter S2 is calculated for the location of the epicenter of the mainshock, with the parameters X and Tlap tuned to maximize the anomaly. The earthquake catalog was that for central California, distributed by the USGS Menlo Park (as used by Zschau). The parameters used were Mmin = 4.5, X = 800 km, an Tlap = 800 days for Landers (a), and Mmin = 3.8, X = 200 km, Tlap = 500 days for the other two cases (b, c). The occurrence times of the mainshocks are marked by stars. In frame (c) both the Chalfant and Landers earthquakes are marked, and each is preceded by an anomaly. 92
